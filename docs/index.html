President's Overview: UL Lafayette at a Glance

This section provides a high-level summary of the University of Louisiana at Lafayette's current strategic landscape, encompassing its mission, key priorities, student demographics, academic strengths, and recent fiscal context. Understanding these foundational elements is crucial for contextualizing the proposed strategies for fiscal health, AI adoption, and enhanced accountability.

Mission & Strategic Priorities (2023-2028)

UL Lafayette's strategic plan focuses on five core priorities:

  • Enhance Student Access, Success, and Experience
  • Advance Faculty and Staff Excellence and Innovation
  • Advance Research, Innovation, and Economic Development
  • Strengthen Stewardship and Operational Effectiveness
  • Elevate Community Engagement and Impact

These priorities aim to drive tangible outcomes like increased retention, graduation rates, and research output.

Student Demographics & Enrollment

Approx. 16,450 students (11,300+ undergrads). Diverse student body with 32% racial-ethnic minorities. Significant population of adult learners. Strategic goal to grow enrollment to 20,000.

Academic Programs & Research Focus

Nationally recognized programs in Nursing and Engineering. Emphasis on expanding interdisciplinary, online, and graduate offerings. Strong focus on research, innovation, and economic development, particularly SDG-related and STEM research.

Key research initiatives include faculty grants for SDGs and NSF-funded programs for STEM diversity.

Recent Fiscal Performance & LLA Challenges

Recurring Louisiana Legislative Auditor (LLA) findings highlight critical areas needing attention:

  • Untimely billing for grants and contracts (3rd consecutive year).
  • Inadequate controls for personnel expenses on Federal R&D awards (4th year).
  • Inadequate monitoring of subrecipients (4th year).
  • Noncompliance with period of performance for Federal R&D awards.

Addressing these is crucial for financial health and compliance, especially with the university citing staff shortages as a factor.

Path to Fiscal Resilience

This section explores innovative fiscal strategies and cost optimization measures essential for UL Lafayette's long-term sustainability and its ability to fund strategic priorities. The focus is on diversifying revenue beyond traditional sources and enhancing operational excellence.

  • Strategic Public-Private Partnerships (PPPs): Leverage external capital for infrastructure, services, and academic programs. Success depends on shared vision, commitment, and communication.
  • Lifelong Learning & Micro-credentials: Tap into adult learner market with high-demand programs, professional training, and flexible online offerings.
  • Intellectual Property (IP) Commercialization: Strategically manage and license university-generated IP through a dedicated Technology Transfer Office.
  • Next-Generation Fundraising: Utilize technology, personalization, and cause-driven narratives to engage diverse donors (CRM Advance is a key tool).
  • Innovative Asset Utilization: Optimize use of physical, intellectual, and human assets (e.g., facility rentals, executive education).
  • Strategic Workforce Management: Rigorous position control, voluntary separation programs, and workforce planning (informed by Cornerstone OnDemand for performance).
  • Process Re-engineering: Redesign inefficient administrative processes (e.g., using Chrome River for expenses, PaymentWorks for suppliers).
  • Shared Services & Automation: Consolidate common functions (IT, HR, Finance) and automate routine tasks.
  • Strategic Procurement: Centralize purchasing, leverage buying power, and use e-procurement systems (informed by PaymentWorks).
  • Energy Efficiency & Space Optimization: Implement energy-saving measures and optimize classroom/facility utilization.

Data-Driven Accountability & Performance

This section focuses on establishing robust Key Performance Indicators (KPIs), leveraging integrated data from Ellucian Banner and other systems (Cornerstone, Chrome River, Maxient, CRMs, etc.), and designing effective executive dashboards to enhance transparency, accountability, and strategic decision-making at UL Lafayette.

Key Performance Indicator (KPI) Framework

A comprehensive KPI framework is vital. KPIs should be SMART and cover:

Financial Health: Operating Margin, Net Tuition Revenue/FTE, Grant Funding.
Enrollment Trends: Application-Yield-Matriculation Funnel, Retention & Graduation Rates.
Student Success: Course Completion, Time to Degree, Post-Graduation Placement.
Operational Efficiency: Cost/Student, Admin Overhead, Classroom Utilization.
Research Activity: R&D Expenditures, Grant Success Rate, Publications.
Advancement: Alumni Giving Rate, Total Funds Raised.

Data drawn from Banner, Cornerstone, Chrome River, Maxient, CRMs, PaymentWorks, Touchnet.

Louisiana Regulatory Environment & Accountability

UL Lafayette operates under ULS and Board of Regents governance, Louisiana's Outcomes-Based Funding Formula (OBFF), and mandates for transparency.

  • OBFF Impact: Funding tied to student progression, completion (especially in high-demand fields), research expenditures, and success of specific student groups (Pell, URM, adult learners).
  • Transparency Mandates: Foreign funding disclosure, extensive BoR reporting, LLA audits.

Strategic Roadmap & Recommendations

This section synthesizes key recommendations for UL Lafayette to harness AI, enhance fiscal health, and strengthen accountability, guiding the university towards its strategic goals.

1. Strengthen Data Governance, Infrastructure & Analytical Capabilities

Establish a formal Data Governance Council. Invest in data infrastructure modernization (leveraging Ellucian Insights). Enhance analytical talent and promote data literacy campus-wide.

2. Prioritize Resolution of LLA Audit Findings

Form an Audit Remediation Task Force. Conduct process re-engineering for grant management, effort reporting, and subrecipient monitoring. Implement technology-enhanced controls and invest in staffing/training for OSP FAC.

3. Align Academic Programs & Research with State Priorities & OBFF

Continuously review programs for market alignment. Strategically invest in OBFF-weighted areas. Focus research growth on state needs and expand public-private partnerships.

4. Enhance Strategic Enrollment Management with Data Analytics

Implement predictive enrollment modeling. Use data-informed financial aid leveraging. Personalize student outreach (using CRM Recruit/Advance) and adapt to market demands for online/adult learners.

5. Foster a Culture of Transparency, Accountability & Continuous Improvement

Implement proactive communication with public-facing dashboards. Empower internal units with data. Link performance to resource allocation. Establish a cycle of continuous improvement.

6. Champion a University-Wide AI Vision & Responsible Deployment

Articulate a clear AI vision. Invest in data preparedness and AI literacy. Launch high-impact AI pilot programs (M365 Copilot, chatbots). Commit to responsible AI practices from the outset.
